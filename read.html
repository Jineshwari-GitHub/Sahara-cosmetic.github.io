SKINCARE

These makeup and skincare
must-have hybrids boast
serious benefits

Yes, there are beauty buys that can give you a photo-ready look
while offering skincaring advantages. Here are
the options ahead of the pack

You may not be likely to say makeup and skincare in the same breath, but you must. After all, if you’re using a full-coverage foundation to
mask your breakouts, some ingredients in the very product may exacerbate your acne. Or your quixotic quest for an eye-opening mascara
can lead to lashes that fall or dry out. The solution? Makeup packed with skincare. While active ingredients in cosmetics rarely reach levels
that render them completely sufficient to treat a serious skin condition on its own, makeup and skincare must-have hybrids promise a number
of benefits. “With a versatile product, you get the dual convenience of makeup and skincare in one. The infusion of super-savvy formulas and
skin-loving ingredients confer colour and coverage, and also act as a protective layer ensuring your skin breathes well on the inside and your
face dazzles on the outside,” shares Samir Modi, founder and managing director of Colorbar. with a rise in innovative products,
separating the good from the great, the better from the best, is harder than ever. So, we have done it for you.

Is mackeup bad for your skin?

The novelty of skin-caring makeup leads one to wonder if traditional makeup is bad for your skin. It’s a complicated question,
but let’s make one thing clear. Classifications like “clean” and “natural” do not mean thatthese formulas are safer for you.
While the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) is yet to provide clarity on what criteria do these claims constitute,
we see them as evidence thatskin-friendly ingredients are at the heart of these formulations. It’s all about understanding the
ingredients inside each product in your routine — just as you would with the food you eat or the ingestible you take.

It is this philosophy that has inspired many of our favourite brand founders to create products full of desirable and easy-to-understand
ingredients. Saie founder and CEO Laney Crowell tells Vogue that she wanted to start her brand to offer makeup products that serve
our bodies. “When you start formulating makeup with really clean ingredients that are good for you, you’re left with skincare,” she says.
